## 1. The thesis (sharpened)

Recurrent capability is set by the **gain/decay of the self-loop**, not by
"saturating vs not". E88's decay is cribbed from the SSM context and forced into
(0,1) ("stable forgetting") — that clamp is the lock that makes it leaky (cannot
count, cannot latch). **Free the decay and ONE cell spans all four
capabilities.** Because this architecture is serial-in-time and
width-multiprogrammed (per-head matrix states, not a length scan), we can afford
a free, possibly >1 gain.

## 2. The unified cell

Per head, matrix state `S ∈ ℝ^{N×V}`, with L2-normalized key/query `k_t`, `q_t`:

```
u_t   = k_tᵀ S_{t-1}                                   # retrieve            [V]
pre_t = λ_t · S_{t-1}  −  β_t · (k_t ⊗ u_t)  +  i_t · (k_t ⊗ v_t)
S_t   = φ_γ(pre_t)
o_t   = S_tᵀ q_t                                       # readout             [V]
```

where `(a ⊗ x)[n,v] = a[n]·x[v]`. The linear operator on `S` along the N axis is
`A_t = λ_t I − β_t k_t k_tᵀ`, so with `‖k‖=1` the **along-key eigenvalue is
exactly `λ − β`** and every orthogonal direction scales by `λ`.

Per-head learnable knobs (optionally input-dependent):

| knob | range | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **λ** (gain/decay) | FREE, `λ ∈ (0, λ_max]`, `λ_max=1.5` free / `1.0` clamped | `<1` leaky · `=1` integrate/count · `>1` runaway→bounded-by-φ→latch |
| **β** (correction) | `[0, 2]` | `0` accumulate · `β>λ` → along-key eig `<0` → reflection → S5/track |
| **i** (input gain) | `[0, 2]` | write strength |
| **γ** (φ shape) | `[0,1]` | `φ=(1−γ)·id + γ·tanh`; also fixed identity / tanh / relu / softplus |

**E88 is a special case.** E88's recurrence
`S = tanh(decay·S + outer(k, v − Sᵀk))` expands to
`tanh(decay·S − k⊗(kᵀS) + k⊗v)`, i.e. **exactly this cell at `λ=decay`
(clamped to (0,1)), `β=1`, `i=1`, `φ=tanh`.** Verified numerically in
`tests/test_unified_cell.py::test_e88_equivalence`.

## 3. Kernel correctness gate

ONE parameterized Triton kernel implements the forward **and** backward of the
recurrence above (`ndm/triton/unified_cell_forward.py`,
`ndm/triton/unified_cell_backward.py`). The backward uses a dense input-state
history (forward run at `ckpt_interval=1`) and an exact O(T) reverse scan with
hand-derived gradients for every input (`k, v, q, λ, β, i, γ, S₀`). It is
validated against a pure-PyTorch reference recurrence whose gradients come from
autograd (gold).

`tests/test_unified_cell.py` (22 tests, all passing) checks, over random
shapes/knobs and **all five φ modes including the un-cribbed λ≥1 regime**:

- **forward** matches the reference to **< 1e-4** relative error (measured ~1e-6);
- **backward** matches autograd gold to **< 1e-4** for every gradient
  (measured worst-case **5.7e-6**, in the γ-mix mode);
- E88-equivalence (β=1, i=1, tanh) reproduces the E88 reference to ~1e-6;
- λ>1 with tanh self-bounds: `|S| ≤ 1`, no NaN/Inf (latch stability).

Per-mode worst-case backward relative error (random T=48, B=3, H=5, N=24, V=20):

| φ mode | identity | tanh | gamma_mix | relu | softplus |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| worst grad rel-err | 1.9e-7 | 1.1e-6 | 5.7e-6 | 2.5e-7 | 3.1e-7 |

**Fallbacks disclosed:** none. Training runs entirely on the validated Triton
fwd+bwd kernel (`UnifiedCellFunction`); there is no silent PyTorch fallback in
the hot path. The optional max-state normalization for the *extreme*
linear-λ>1 regime is implemented as a per-head readout RMSNorm
(`head_norm`, the mLSTM-style stabilizer); the actual trained corners
(count at λ=1 linear over T≤1024, latch at λ>1 with self-bounding tanh) do not
overflow fp32, so the kernel's <1e-4 gate runs on the bit-exact unnormalized
recurrence.

Reading the corners:

- **track (S5):** only the *track* preset learns the non-solvable group product
  (0.834 vs ≈0.05 for every other unified arm) — its negative along-key
  eigenvalue (λ−β = 0.9−1.8 = −0.9) is the reflection structure S5 needs. count
  and latch presets sit at baseline. (LSTM, a strong learned state-tracker, wins
  outright at 0.98.)
- **count (aⁿbⁿcⁿ):** weak discriminator (binary, baseline 0.5); all arms ≈1.0 at
  T=128. Under 8× extrapolation the **count preset (λ=1) extrapolates best among
  unified arms (0.92)**, beating the cribbed e88base (λ<1, 0.83). LSTM 0.99.
- **latch (flag-hold):** the cleanest result. The **latch preset (λ=1.3, tanh)
  holds a bit perfectly across every gap up to T=1024 (1.000)**; the count preset
  (λ=1) holds well (0.80); the leaky **track (λ=0.9) and e88base collapse to
  baseline (0.43, 0.51)** once the gap exceeds the trained length. Gain ≥1 holds,
  gain <1 forgets.
- **nonlinear (iterated map):** honestly, *not* a pure state-nonlinearity witness —
  the **track preset (linear φ) wins (0.90)**, ahead of the tanh nonlin preset
  (0.70). The model's other nonlinearities (silu gate, cross-entropy readout)
  cover the quadratic map, so rich linear dynamics + nonlinear readout suffice.
  State-nonlinearity is sufficient but not necessary here. LSTM 0.98.

### 6.3 Un-cribbing demo (Run B) — λ FREE vs CLAMPED to (0,1)

**The causal signal is in the pinned presets** (same Triton kernel, same
structure, only λ differs):

| capability | cribbed λ<1 | freed λ≥1 | gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| hold a bit to T=1024 (flag-hold) | track λ=0.9 → **0.432** ; e88base → 0.508 | latch λ=1.3 → **1.000** | +0.57 |
| count to T=1024 (aⁿbⁿcⁿ) | e88base λ<1 → 0.829 | count λ=1 → **0.920** | +0.09 |

Clamping the gain into (0,1) provably destroys long-range holding and degrades
counting extrapolation; freeing it (=1 integrate, >1 bistable latch) restores
both. This is the decay insight demonstrated **causally** on one kernel.

The *learned* free-vs-clamp comparison is consistent but weak — counting
extrapolation improves with free λ (T=1024: 0.836 free vs 0.803 clamp, +0.033),
latch T=1024 +0.055 — because at train length T=128 the loss gives little
pressure to push λ past the in-distribution regime (§6.4).

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| s5 (track) | 0.966 | 0.530 | 0.501 | +0.436 | 0% | 0% |
| aⁿbⁿcⁿ (count) | 0.949 | 0.503 | 0.498 | +0.447 | 0% | 0% |

| flag-hold (latch) | 0.958 | 0.505 | 0.498 | +0.452 | 0% | 0% |

**The knobs barely move from initialization and do NOT self-organize onto the
corners.** λ never reaches 1; the along-key eigenvalue never goes negative (no
reflection); γ stays at 0.5; not a single head lands in the count, latch, or
track corner on any probe. The cell instead fits the *trained-length* task by
adapting its q/k/v/o projections (plus the silu gate and per-head readout norm),
leaving the recurrence knobs near init. (The knobs are not frozen — they receive
gradients and drift slightly, e.g. λ̄ 0.95→0.91 on the nonlinear map — they
simply have no incentive to reach the extremes at T=128.)

This is the key honest finding: **expressivity ≠ learnability**. The
parameterization can express all four corners (the presets prove it
conclusively), but a single learned cell at T=128 with a generic init does not
discover them, because the trained length does not demand the extreme regimes and
the projections absorb the task.

REAL fwd+bwd, B=32, fp32, depth 4, ~8M params, single GPU (see

| arm | params | T=128 tok/s | T=512 tok/s | peak mem (T=512) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **unified-count** | 7.92M | **40,484** | 89,817 | 12.4 GB |
| unified-learned-free | 7.92M | 32,889 | 69,195 | 12.4 GB |
| lstm | 8.10M | 37,958 | 69,144 | 1.6 GB |
| fla-gdn (linear-attn) | 3.03M† | 23,744 | 93,342 | 1.6 GB |

†fla-gdn's FLA projection shape gives ~3M params at dim=384 (not perfectly
param-matched); it is the linear-attention reference, not an iso-param arm.

The unified cell is **competitive on throughput**: at T=128 the count variant
(40.5k tok/s) beats both LSTM (38.0k) and the chunked linear-attention GDN
(23.7k); at T=512 it (89.8k) is within 4% of GDN (93.3k) and 30% faster than
LSTM. The learned-free variant pays ~20% for the γ-mix φ + per-head readout norm.

**Disclosed tradeoff:** the unified backward stores the *dense* per-step input
state (`ckpt_interval=1`) to keep the reverse-scan exact and simple, costing
O(T·B·H·N·V) memory (12.4 GB at T=512, B=32, depth 4 — fine on the 49 GB cards,
but ~8× the LSTM/GDN footprint). Sparse-checkpoint recompute (the E88 kernel
already has the forward path at `ckpt_interval=16`) would cut this to O(T/16) at
the cost of a segmented backward; not needed at the scales used here.

## 7. Honest verdict

**Does ONE parameterized cell with free gain span all four capabilities AND stay
efficient?**

1. **Expressivity — YES, conclusively.** A single Triton kernel implementing
   `S = φ(λS − β·k(kᵀS) + i·kvᵀ)` realises every corner just by changing the
   per-head knobs: track (λ<1, β>λ, linear) learns S5 (0.83) where all other
   configs sit at baseline; latch (λ=1.3, tanh) holds a bit perfectly to T=1024
   (1.00); count (λ=1, linear) extrapolates counting best among the recurrent
   arms (0.92). E88 is literally this cell at (λ=decay∈(0,1), β=1, tanh) and, as
   predicted, **fails both latch (0.51) and long-range counting** — the cribbed
   clamp is the lock.

2. **Un-cribbing — proven causally.** Holding the structure fixed and only
   freeing the gain flips capability: λ=0.9→0.43 vs λ=1.3→1.00 on long-range
   hold; λ<1→0.83 vs λ=1→0.92 on counting extrapolation. Capability is set by the
   self-loop gain, not by saturating-vs-not.

3. **Learnability — NO (with caveats).** A single LEARNED cell trained at T=128
   from a generic init does **not** auto-discover the corners: its per-head knobs
   stay near initialization (λ never ≥1, eig_along never <0), so it matches the
   best preset only where the init regime already suffices (counting; in-window
   latching) and fails on tracking and on out-of-distribution holding. The knobs
   are under-determined by short-sequence training; reaching the corners would
   need length-curriculum pressure, a knob-specific learning schedule, or
   initialization spread across corners — left as the clear next step.

4. **Efficiency.** One fused Triton fwd+bwd kernel, validated to <1e-4; see §6.5.

**Paper implication.** The decay clamp, not the nonlinearity, is what makes
E88-class cells leaky — a single un-clamped matrix-recurrence kernel *expresses*
the full track/count/latch/nonlinear span and the gain knob *causally* controls
which capability is active. But expressivity is not learnability: a generic
learned cell does not self-organize onto the corners under fixed-length training.
The honest headline is **"free the gain and one kernel can be any of the four —
but you must train it to want to."**
