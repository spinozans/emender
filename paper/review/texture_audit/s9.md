## Global-voice note (stance calibration, from one read of the whole paper)

The paper speaks as a disciplined empiricist reporting a single demonstration
run plus a machine-checked separation result, with an unusually explicit
claim / scope / non-claim apparatus (the §1 contributions list, the repeated
"honest null" and "the omission is scope, not result" moves). At its best the
voice manufactures context for an outside reader and names the boundary a
result does *not* cross. But two failure registers recur: (1) *insider vantage*
— undefined operational shorthand ("stitched" days, "the racer", "on the rack")
and inline Lean lemma identifiers that presume the reader has been in the lab or
is auditing the trusted core; and (2) *result-as-product* — "production
instance / production architecture", "released v0.3 checkpoints", which dress
one training run as a deployed, versioned software line with a maintenance
cadence. The dominant risk in §9 specifically is not anthropomorphism but a
limitations section that occasionally *argues* (re-establishing robustness and
refuting an absent skeptic) where it should simply state scope and stop.
