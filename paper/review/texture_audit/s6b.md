## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

The paper speaks as a careful, defensive empiricist addressing a skeptical peer-reviewer inside the recurrent-LM subfield. It treats its own codenames — E88, M²RNN-CMA, GDN, "the Emender," "the delta update," "v0.3," "stitched," "racer," "production instance" — as shared currency the reader already holds, rather than manufacturing them. Its dominant tic is pre-emptive self-litigation: nearly every result is wrapped in a named objection it then defeats ("Honest null," "Honest mirror," "fixes the asymmetry," "the asymmetry objection," "a critic can always ask"). And it persistently narrates a single training run (E88) as a shipped product with a "production" pipeline and "deployed" weights — an ontology its own §1 explicitly denies ("E88 is the 1.3 B *instance* evaluated throughout"). The prose is dense, hedge-heavy, and argues with an absent adversary even where the data would stand on its own.
