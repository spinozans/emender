## Global-voice note (calibration, whole-paper read)

The paper speaks in the voice of the architecture's builder addressing an ML-research audience, and it is *mostly* outsider-disciplined: §2 manufactures the background a stranger needs, and the §1 claim/evidence/scope/non-claim table is unusually honest scope-setting. But the stance is punctured in two recurring ways. First, **deployment/biography leakage**: E88 is repeatedly framed as a *deployed* artifact with a history — "production instance," "production stack," "deployed 1.3 B scale," "stitched wall-clock days" — when ontologically it is one training run demonstrating a possibility. Second, a **defensive cadence**: scope, nulls, and non-claims are pre-stated densely and sometimes re-litigated, occasionally arguing with an absent skeptic mid-paragraph rather than confining defense to the explicit non-claim structure. The result is a text that knows the outsider-vantage discipline and follows it at the section level, yet keeps slipping into the project's own world at the sentence level. My unit sits at the calmest, most technical part of the paper (the update equation), so its defects are fewer and milder than the intro's — but the same two leaks appear here in miniature.
