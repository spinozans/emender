## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

Across the whole paper the voice speaks from *inside* its own program to a reader presumed already resident there: proper nouns and codenames (E88, the Emender, M²RNN-CMA, "the racer", "stitched") arrive as shared history, and the result-object E88 — one training run — is repeatedly handed a product biography it does not have ("production instance", "deployed", "released production weights"). The posture is markedly defensive: numbers come pre-read, the prose vouches for its own honesty, and an absent skeptic is answered mid-clause rather than in the explicit scope/non-claim scaffolding the paper built for exactly that purpose. Underneath the texture film the technical reporting is confident and well-ordered. §8 is, notably, **clean of the paper's signature R2 thread** — no "production"/"deployed" leak appears here — but it is the predicted concentrate of a *different* disease: competitor-framing **PRE-LITIGATION** (R3). Two upstream auditors (s2, s6a) explicitly punted the M²RNN-rivalry relitigation into §8, and it is here: the related work reads less as a map of the field than as a sequence of rivals escorted out of contention — by timestamp (GDN-2), by emphasis (M²RNN's "hybrid form"), and three times by a §1 criterion they "violate"/"fail".
