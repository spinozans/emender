## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

The paper speaks as an insider reporting from inside its own experimental program to a reader it presumes already lives there: proper nouns (E88, the Emender, M²RNN-CMA, "the racer", "the campaign") arrive as shared history, and the central result-object E88 is repeatedly handed a biography and a deployment context it does not have ("production instance", "deployed", "E88 invites…"). The voice is also markedly defensive — it tells the reader how to read each number, vouches for its own honesty ("the honest claim", "Honest null", "Honest mirror"), and pre-empts an absent skeptic, though much of that defense is at least corralled into explicit scope/non-claim scaffolding rather than woven into every clause. Underneath the texture film the engineering and mathematical reporting is confident and well-ordered; topic sentences are clean. §7 is, by the paper's standards, one of the *cleaner* sections on ontology — it correctly treats "the Emender" as a formal update family and "E88" as a signature/instance, so the R2 "result-as-system" disease that infects §1/§3/§4 mostly recedes here. The distinctive §7 defect is a different one: the section twice leaks its own internal editorial/QA process into the published surface, addressing the project's collaborators rather than the reader.
