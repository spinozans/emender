## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

The paper speaks from inside its own experimental program to a reader it presumes already lives there: proper nouns (E88, Emender, M²RNN-CMA, "the racer") arrive as shared history rather than as objects the text builds for an outsider, and the central result-object E88 is repeatedly handed a deployment biography — it is the "production instance/stack" of a system that, by the paper's own ontology (R2), is one training run, not a deployed product. The voice runs faintly defensive throughout: it tells the reader how to *read* each number, vouches for its own honesty ("Honest null", "Honest mirror"), and pre-empts skeptical objections inside the same passage that makes the claim instead of confining defense to the explicit scope/non-claim table built in §1. Beneath the texture film the engineering is confident and the topic-sentence ordering mostly sound, so the defects are a stance film over a solid skeleton, not structural collapse.
