## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

The paper speaks from inside its own experimental program to a reader it presumes already inhabits that world: proper nouns (E88, the Emender, M²RNN-CMA, "the racer", "the campaign", "production") arrive as shared history rather than as objects the text builds for an outsider, and results are handed biographies and agency (E88 "invites" programs, "reaches", has a "production" tier). The voice is intermittently defensive — it tells the reader how to read a number, vouches for its own honesty/fairness, and pre-empts skeptical objections inside the sentence that makes the claim, instead of confining defense to the explicit scope/non-claim table set up in §1. Underneath the film, the engineering reporting is clean and confident: topic sentences are crisp and the technical ordering is mostly sound. So the defects are a texture layer over a solid skeleton — stance slippage and unpaid insider words, not structural failure.
