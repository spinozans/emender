## Global-voice note (stance calibration, from one read of the whole paper)

The paper speaks as a careful empiricist reporting one demonstration run and a
machine-checked separation result, with an unusually disciplined claim/scope/
non-claim apparatus (the §1 claims table, the repeated "honest null" framing).
At its best the voice manufactures context for an outside reader and states the
boundary a result does *not* cross. But the voice leaks project-internal idiom
in two registers: undefined operational shorthand ("stitched" days, "the
racer") that presumes the reader has been in the lab, and a habit of citing
exact Lean lemma identifiers and the `sorry`/`admit` cleanliness checklist as
if the reader is auditing the trusted core rather than reading a paper. E88 is
mostly handled correctly as a result/artifact, though "production instance"
(elsewhere) and the recap density push it toward sounding like a deployed
system. The dominant texture risk is not anthropomorphism but *insider
vantage*: the text occasionally addresses a colleague who already shares the
nicknames and the build state.
