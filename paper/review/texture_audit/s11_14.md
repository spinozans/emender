## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance calibration)

The paper speaks as an insider reporting from inside its own multi-year experimental program to a reader it presumes already lives in that world: proper nouns (E88, the Emender, M²RNN-CMA, the codename milestones E63/E70–E75, "the racer") arrive as shared history rather than as objects the text constructs for an outsider, and result-objects are repeatedly handed biographies and agency — most visibly the recurring epithet "production," which imports a deployment/product tier that does not exist (E88 is one training run). The voice is also faintly defensive: it tells the reader how to *read* a number, vouches for its own honesty, and pre-empts the skeptic inside the sentence that makes the claim. Underneath this is sound, sober engineering reporting with clean topic sentences, so the defects are a texture film, not structural failure. My unit is the paper's tail and is genuinely texture-low: §11 is in fact a model of the posture the rest of the paper should hold (state prediction, state falsifier, move on), and §12/§13/§14 are mostly disciplined documentation — the residual defects are the paper-wide "production" epithet recurring in the lineage appendix and a clustered don't-misread-the-table defensiveness in the bpb caption that echoes the occupancy relitigation other auditors will find in §4.
