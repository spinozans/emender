## Global-voice note (whole-paper stance)

The paper speaks as a project insider who already lives inside E88's world and assumes the reader does too: the first sentence of the intro opens with the bare codename "E88" and the unpaid word "production," and codenames (E88, M²RNN-CMA, GDN), insider nouns ("racer," "stitched" days), and a "production instance" framing are treated as shared history rather than manufactured for an outsider. The stance toward the reader is persistently defensive — the prose repeatedly anticipates and rebuts an absent skeptic ("not seed-luck," "not a buried caveat," "we measure it directly rather than assert it") even where the explicit Claim/Evidence/Scope/Non-claim table already houses that defense. There is a real tension in the voice between two registers: a disciplined scope-stating scientist (the claims table, the explicit Finding/Caveat/Scope blocks) and an anxious advocate who relitigates the same caveat three or four times in adjacent sentences. A third register leaks anthropomorphism — memory that "can change its mind" / "stand firm," a search that "voted" — treating a single training result (E88) as an entity with temperament and biography rather than as one run demonstrating a possibility. For an outside reader who knows nothing of an Emender, the paper frequently states results and uses project vocabulary before manufacturing the context that would make either legible.
