# SPECIALIZATION STUDY — can pressure (not placement) force a single cell to cover all four corners?

**Task:** `specialization-study`. Builds on
[UNIFIED_CELL_RESULTS.md](UNIFIED_CELL_RESULTS.md) (expressivity: one un-clamped
matrix-recurrence kernel `S = φ(λS − β·k(kᵀS) + i·kvᵀ)` realises every capability
corner — track / count / latch / nonlinear — by changing its per-head knobs) and
[UNIFIED_LEARNABILITY_RESULTS.md](UNIFIED_LEARNABILITY_RESULTS.md) (a generic-init
LEARNED cell does NOT self-organise onto the corners; **init-spread + knob-LR**
fixes it).

That prior fix was an inductive **placement** (spread the knobs across corners at
init). This study asks the complementary question: **can a specialization
*pressure* — a regulariser, or a shared type-dictionary — force a single LEARNED
cell to self-organise into a heterogeneous all-corner head mixture from a generic
center, *without* hand-placing the knobs?** This is the question of HORIZONTAL
head-type hybridization: heterogeneous head TYPES inside ONE layer.

Three families of approach, all on the **same validated Triton kernel (no kernel
rewrite; 22/22 kernel tests still pass)**:

1. **Specialization-pressure regulariser** (primary) on a **generic-init** learned
   cell — the cell that collapsed to center. Variants:
   - **pull** (a): per-head pull-to-nearest-corner, `mean_h min_c d²(h,c)`.
   - **anticenter** (b): repel the center / reward being near ANY corner (bounded
     Gaussian bumps `exp(−d²_center) − exp(−d²_corner)`).
   - **coverage** (c): population diversity, `KL(p‖uniform)` of the
     head→nearest-corner assignment distribution (anti-collapse).
   - **pull_cov** = a+c, **anticenter_cov** = b+c.
   - Weight swept ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 1.0}, annealed in over the first half of training.
2. **Type-dictionary**: K shared LEARNABLE prototype knobs + a per-head soft
   weight over prototypes (`knob_h = Σ_k softmax(w_h)_k · proto_k`). K ∈ {4, 8}.
   Prototypes init spread across corners; soft weights init near-uniform.
3. **Fixed-type-population** (floor): heads HARD-assigned to the four corners as
   non-learnable buffers; only the q/k/v/o projections + gate learn.

**References** (reused, not re-run): generic-init learned, spread-init+knob-LR
(klr20, the best prior arm), LSTM, single-corner presets.

REAL training only, fp32, schedule-free AdamW, ~7.93M params, depth 4, 32 heads,
N=V=32, idle-GPU-only scheduler. 3 seeds {42,123,456}; train T=128; eval (length
extrapolation) at T∈{128,256,512,1024}. **270/270 runs completed, 0 failures.**
`paper/main.typ` untouched.

## 0. Headline — pressure does NOT replace placement

**A single learned cell does NOT self-organise into a diverse all-corner head
mixture under any pressure we tested. Coverage of the four corners is achieved
ONLY by inductive *placement* of the knobs — either hard fixed-assignment
(`fixedpop`) or spread-init+knob-LR (prior work). The regulariser and the
type-dictionary both fail.** Concretely, on the MIXED probe (all four
capabilities at once), classifying each head to its nearest corner centroid:

*[auto]* (MIXED probe, per-head final knobs pooled over 384 heads × 3 seeds)

| approach | %heads on a real corner | corners covered | mixed acc@128 | mixed acc@1024 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| generic-init learned (ref) | **0%** | **0/4** | 0.538 | 0.478 |
| regulariser — *best of 15 arms* | 0% (pressure too weak) † | 0/4 | 0.54 | ≤0.52 |
| type-dictionary (K=4 / K=8) | **0%** | **0/4** | 0.52 | 0.47 |
| **fixed-type-population (floor)** | **100%** | **4/4** | **0.784** | **0.547** |
| spread-init+knob-LR klr20 (ref) | **100%** | **4/4** | **0.922** | **0.719** |

† The single exception, `anticenter` at weight 1.0, pushes 67% of heads OFF the
center — but they **all collapse onto one corner** (nonlin), 1/4 coverage, not a
diverse mixture (§3). Every other regulariser arm leaves 100% of heads at center.

So the honest answer to the task's framing: **the cheapest intervention (a
regulariser) is NOT enough, and the type-dictionary is NOT enough. Fixed-type
assignment — or its learnable cousin, spread-init — is required.** Among the two
working (placement) methods, **spread-init+knob-LR > fixed-population**: both cover
all four corners, but the learnable-but-placed knobs extrapolate markedly better
(mixed 0.719 vs 0.547 @1024; S5 0.541 vs 0.142 @1024) because they can *refine*
each corner, whereas the fixed knobs cannot.

## 2. Specialization — do heads cover the four corners?

*[auto]* MIXED probe, per-head final knobs classified to the nearest of five
centroids {track, count, latch, nonlin, center}. "%held" = nearest is a real
corner; "covered" = real corners with ≥5% of heads (4 = full coverage).

| approach | %held | %center | %eig<0 | covered | track | count | latch | nonlin | center |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| generic (ref) | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| spread-klr20 (ref) | 100% | 0% | 30% | **4/4** | 94 | 96 | 96 | 98 | 0 |
| reg-pull (all w) | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| reg-coverage (all w) | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| reg-pull_cov (all w) | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| reg-anticenter_cov (all w) | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| reg-anticenter-w1 | 67% | 33% | 0% | **1/4** | 0 | 0 | 0 | 257 | 127 |
| unified-dict4 | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| unified-dict8 | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0/4 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 |
| **unified-fixedpop** | 100% | 0% | 25% | **4/4** | 96 | 96 | 96 | 96 | 0 |

**Why the regulariser fails — it cannot escape the center basin.** Generic init
sits exactly at center (λ̄≈0.95, β̄≈0.5, γ̄≈0.5). Tracking the final per-head means
on the mixed task:

| reg arm (w=1) | final λ̄ | final β̄ | final γ̄ | final reg-loss | result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| pull        | 0.902 | 0.524 | 0.637 | 0.099 (↓ from ≈0.30) | nudged, still center |
| coverage    | 0.921 | 0.634 | 0.411 | 0.555 (barely ↓)     | stuck at center |
| pull_cov    | 0.938 | 0.564 | 0.438 | 0.863                | stuck at center |
| anticenter_cov | 0.913 | 0.542 | 0.444 | 1.044             | stuck at center |
| anticenter  | 0.808 | 0.535 | 0.650 | −0.007              | escapes → ONE corner |

The per-head pull *does* reduce its loss (0.30→0.10 at w=1) but the knobs only
drift a few hundredths and every head stays inside the center's classification
basin. Two compounding reasons: (i) **weight decay (0.01) on the raw knobs pulls
sigmoid⁻¹ toward 0 → λ,β,γ toward their mid-range = the center**, directly
opposing specialization; and (ii) the input-dependent q/k/v/o projections already
absorb the trained-length task (the prior study's finding), so the task loss gives
the knobs almost no gradient and the regulariser is fighting weight-decay alone.
The combined variants (`*_cov`) split a fixed weight budget across two terms and
end with the HIGHEST reg-loss of all — the coverage and per-head terms partially
cancel, leaving the knobs pinned at center. Only raw `anticenter` at full weight
overcomes weight-decay, but with no per-corner target it sends every escaping head
to the *same* nearest exit (the nonlin corner), giving collapse, not coverage.

**Why the dictionary fails — shared gradient collapses the prototypes.** The K
prototypes init spread on the corners, but the *shared* gradient (every head pulls
every prototype) drags them together; they converge to ≈(λ 1.0, β 0.04, γ 0.6),
and with near-uniform soft weights every head's effective knob lands on that
single near-center point (0/4 covered). The very property that was meant to give
"strong shared gradient" is what destroys per-head diversity.

## 3. Emergent head-type mixture per task

*[auto]* For the approaches that DO specialise, the learned mixture is a **flat
~25/25/25/25 four-type population on EVERY probe** — including the single-corner
probes:

| approach | probe | track | count | latch | nonlin | center | covered |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **fixedpop** | s5 / count / nonlin / latch / mixed | 96 | 96 | 96 | 96 | 0 | 4/4 (all) |
| **spread-klr20** | s5 | 96 | 96 | 96 | 96 | 0 | 4/4 |
| spread-klr20 | iter-map | 95 | 96 | 95 | 98 | 0 | 4/4 |
| spread-klr20 | mixed | 94 | 96 | 96 | 98 | 0 | 4/4 |
| reg-* (representative) | every probe | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 | 0/4 |
| dict4 / dict8 | every probe | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 384 | 0/4 |

The horizontal-hybridization finding: **the working approaches keep a balanced
four-type head population in every layer regardless of task, and route the active
capability through the q/k/v/o projections rather than by re-shaping the
population.** Even on a pure S5 (track-only) task, fixedpop and spread keep 96
count/latch/nonlin heads each — the unused types are simply gated/projected out,
not converted. There is no task-dependent re-balancing of head TYPES; the mixture
is set by placement (init or fixed) and held. The regulariser/dictionary produce
no mixture at all (all heads at center on every probe).

nearly free (fixedpop is actually −512 params; spread is +0). The deciding factor
is **where the knobs start / are pinned**, not how many there are.

## 5. Head-to-head verdict

**Which approach yields a SINGLE learnable model that (i) covers all four corners
and (ii) shows interpretable head specialization — and at what cost?**

Ranked, on the mixed all-capabilities task:

1. **spread-init + knob-LR (prior work) — BEST.** 4/4 coverage, 100% held, top
   accuracy (mixed 0.922@128 / 0.719@1024; S5 1.000 / 0.541), +0 params. Learnable
   knobs that are *placed* on the corners and then refined.
2. **fixed-type-population — the robust FLOOR, and it is required if you insist on
   not placing/refining learnable knobs.** 4/4 coverage by construction, second-best
   accuracy (mixed 0.784@128 / 0.547@1024; S5 0.995@128), −512 params. Loses to
   spread only on extrapolation, because its knobs can't refine.
3. **regulariser — FAILS.** +0 params, but no coverage (0/4) and accuracy pinned to
   the generic-init failure. Pressure cannot overcome weight-decay + projection
   absorption from a center start; the strongest setting collapses to one corner.
4. **type-dictionary — FAILS, slightly worse than generic.** Shared gradient
   collapses the prototypes; 0/4 coverage and a small accuracy regression.

**Does the regulariser alone suffice (cheapest)?** **No.** **Is the dictionary
needed / sufficient?** **No — it is also insufficient.** **Is fixed assignment
required?** For a cell trained from a generic center with *no* placed init, **yes
— fixed-type-population is the answer** (it is the only structural method here that
covers all corners). But the prior study already showed a *learnable* model can
cover all corners — via spread-**init** (a one-time placement, not a fixed
assignment), which then extrapolates better than the fixed floor.

**The clean statement for the paper.** *Horizontal head-type hybridization in one
unified cell is governed by inductive **placement**, not by training **pressure**.
A specialization regulariser and a shared type-dictionary both fail to pull a
generic-center cell onto a diverse all-corner mixture — weight-decay and the
input-dependent projections keep the recurrence knobs at the center, and a
strong-enough push collapses them onto a single corner rather than spreading. The
heterogeneous four-type mixture appears only when the knobs are **placed** on the
corners (spread-init, which then refines and extrapolates best) or **pinned** there
(fixed-type-population, the robust floor). Capacity and pressure were never the
missing ingredient; placement was.*
