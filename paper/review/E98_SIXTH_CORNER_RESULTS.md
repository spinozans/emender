# E98 SIXTH-CORNER — add the GATED-DELTA backbone: is the GDN recall+track regime reachable inside the E98 cell?

**Task:** `e98-sixth-corner`. Builds on
[E98_FIVE_CORNER_RESULTS.md](E98_FIVE_CORNER_RESULTS.md), which located the real
recall workhorse: leaky-linear is the *weakest* recall corner, while **GDN solved
MQAR 0.951** (4.6× fewer params) and **GDN+neg-eig** won S5 length-robustly — so
the genuine LM backbone is **gated-delta** (clean-overwrite + gated decay), and no
placed corner occupied it. This study adds it as a 6th corner and asks, honestly:
**is the GDN operating point reachable inside the validated E98 cell, and does a
6-type population get real recall?**

The gated-delta corner is the GDN operating point expressed in the **VALIDATED E98
split-gated unified-cell kernel** (`pre = λ_t·S − β·k((b⊙k)ᵀS) + i·k(w⊙v)ᵀ`,
`S = φ_γ(pre)`) — **no kernel rewrite**:

- **β≈1** — full delta / clean overwrite;
- **φ = identity** — linear state;
- **input-dependent gated decay λ_t ∈ (0,1)** (Mamba/GDN style, **not** a fixed λ),
  computed from the input by a new per-head **decay gate** and fed through the
  kernel's *existing* time-varying `lam_t` input (the kernel already consumed `lam`
  as `[T,B,H]`; we only repurpose that — **the 47/47-passing kernel is untouched**);
- along-key eigenvalue = `λ_t − β` ∈ (−1, 0]: **≈0** clean erase-then-write (recall)
  when the gate holds λ_t≈1, **negative** (reflection / S5 track) when λ_t<1 — so the
  same corner can also reflect, the **neg-eig variant**.

> REAL training only, no mocks. fp32 for every unified arm; the GDN references run
> bf16 (their FLA chunk-delta kernels reject float32). schedule-free AdamW, depth 4,
> dim 256, 32 heads, N=V=32 (~7.4M params), spread-init + `knob_lr_mult=20`. 3 seeds
> {42,123,456}; train T=128; eval (length extrapolation) at T∈{128,256,512,1024}.
> **69/69 runs completed, 0 failures.** Co-located idle-GPU scheduler (used-mem <
> 38 GB on the 48 GB cards; never preempt — ran in parallel with cma-capability).

## 0. Headline — honest verdict

**The premise is confirmed at the level of *ranking* and refuted at the level of
*reachability*. The GDN recall regime is NOT reachable by placing a gated-delta
operating point inside the E98 cell.**

1. **The gated-delta PRESET does NOT close the recall gap to GDN.** On MQAR @128:
   **gated-delta 0.171 vs GDN 0.951 — a −0.78 gap that does not close.** It *does*
   beat the leaky corner (+0.060: 0.171 vs 0.111), confirming the task's premise
   that *full-delta gated-delta is a better recall corner than fading leaky-linear*.
   But "better than the worst corner" ≠ "the GDN workhorse." gated-delta lands
   **mid-pack** among the unified single-corner presets (latch 0.215 > track 0.165
   ≈ **gated-delta 0.171** > nonlin 0.143 > count 0.136 > leaky 0.111). **GDN's
   recall is an architectural property (feature maps, normalisation, chunked
   parallel delta), not an (β, λ_t, φ) operating point you can place** — even with
   genuinely input-dependent decay.

2. **A 6-type population gets NO real recall over a 5- or 4-type one.** At T=128,
   **spread-6 ties spread-5 on all six probes (0 wins / 0 regressions)**, and on
   recall it is *flat-to-slightly-lower* than both priors: spread-4 0.440, spread-5
   0.271, **spread-6 0.261**. The spread-4 lead is a **variance artefact** (seeds
   [0.45, 0.69, 0.18]); the dedicated recall corners actually *tightened* the
   distribution (spread-6 [0.30, 0.24, 0.24]) at a slightly lower mean. **Placing a
   gated-delta corner did not give the population real recall** — it remains ~0.26,
   3.6× below GDN, and collapses to ≈0.03 at 8× length while GDN holds 0.26.

3. **gated-delta does NOT serve as a homogeneous recall+track backbone either.** As
   a single-type preset it is weak at *both* recall (0.171) *and* S5 (0.179 @128,
   collapsing to 0.03 @1024). The population's S5 success (spread-6 1.000 @128) comes
   entirely from the **track** corner, not gated-delta. The dual recall+track backbone
   that actually exists is **GDN itself** (gdn 0.951 recall, gdn-neg 0.998 S5).

4. **The placed gated-delta heads hold their placement but DRIFT off the
   clean-overwrite point.** 0 % collapse-to-center on the exotic probes; but the
   gated-delta group learns **β≈1.2–1.4, eig ≈ −0.2 to −0.4** — i.e. it slides from
   clean-overwrite (eig≈0) toward **reflection/track** (negative eig), not toward
   recall. On MQAR the recall load is carried by **leaky (27 %) + nonlin (25 %) +
   track (25 %)** heads, with gated-delta only **6 %**.

**Bottom line for the scale population:** *do not build the backbone from a placed
gated-delta corner expecting GDN recall — it is not reachable this way.* The correct
hybrid is the **inverse**: use an **actual GDN / gated-delta layer as the backbone**
(4.6× smaller, and it solves *both* recall 0.95 and S5 0.998) with a **sprinkle of
unified exotic-corner specialists** for the counting / latch / nonlinear primitives
GDN does not natively do. The sixth corner is *harmless to add* and is a *strictly
better recall corner than leaky*, but it is **not** the LM workhorse inside the cell.

## 5. Interpretation — why the GDN regime is not reachable

The gated-delta operating point — β≈1, identity φ, input-dependent λ_t — is
*structurally* GDN-like, and we verified the decay is genuinely input-dependent (real
unit test: λ_t varies with the input, stays in (0,λ_max], routes gradient into the
decay gate). Yet the preset gets **mid-pack 0.17 recall**, not GDN's 0.95. Three
reasons, consistent with the data:

1. **Recall is architectural, not operational.** GDN's MQAR strength comes from its
   *full* design — SiLU feature maps, L2-normalised keys/values, the specific
   chunked parallel delta with its normalisation — not from the scalar (β, λ_t, φ)
   regime. The E98 cell at the same operating point lacks those, and tops out where
   every unified single corner does (~0.1–0.2). This *extends and confirms* the
   five-corner finding (GDN 0.951 with 4.6× fewer params) rather than overturning it.

2. **A homogeneous gated-delta population is interference-limited.** All-delta heads
   over-write each other's bindings; the mixed populations (spread-4/5/6) do *better*
   at recall (0.26–0.44) than the pure gated-delta preset (0.17), the opposite of a
   "place the workhorse and win" story.

3. **Gradient does not want clean-overwrite for recall.** The placed gated-delta
   heads drift to negative eig (§4b) — descent uses them as reflectors, not as
   clean key-value stores. The recall that the population *does* achieve is carried
   by a *mixture* (leaky + nonlin + track), not by a single delta corner.
