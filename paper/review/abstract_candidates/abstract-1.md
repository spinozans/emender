A single workstation-class GPU is enough. A recurrent language model whose state passes through a nonlinearity at every step, which forecloses the parallel scan that linear recurrences rely on for throughput, reaches below one bit per byte on The Pile. The throughput comes from width: hundreds of small recurrent programs per token, each running its time loop serially. The measurement is held out. There, bulk loss goes quiet. A delta-correcting model, a raw-write model, and a linear-recurrent model stand in a statistical tie, and language-model bits do not separate them. The separation is elsewhere, on two axes. Computability: the linear recurrence cannot track non-solvable group state at length, a converged ceiling, not undertraining. Efficiency: at matched budget the delta-correcting update generalizes on that state far past its training length, while the raw-write update, which in principle can, under-reaches. A Lean 4 trusted core proves the delta update reaches a strictly larger one-step function class than raw-write at matched compute.

Seed: stillness, one disturbance, then stillness; angle: the held-out bulk-loss tie as the quiet, with the two-axis state-tracking separation as the only real sound.
