# CMA STAGE 1 — tuning the E98 unified-cell META-CONFIG on a multi-capability fitness

**Task:** `cma-capability`. Builds on
[SPECIALIZATION_STUDY_RESULTS.md](SPECIALIZATION_STUDY_RESULTS.md) (horizontal
head-type hybridization is governed by inductive **placement**, not training

[E98_ON_E97_RESULTS.md](E98_ON_E97_RESULTS.md) (the E97 split-gate is a strict

## 0. Headline — CMA tuning of the meta-config beats the hand-set form, at fewer params

**A CMA-tuned 8M meta-config of the winning E98 form covers all four capability
corners AND the mixed all-capabilities task markedly better than the hand-set
baseline — and does so at 27% FEWER parameters than the 11.07M config
E98_ON_E97 emitted.** Multi-capability fitness = mean length-extrapolation
accuracy over the four corner probes (s5 / aⁿbⁿcⁿ / iterated-nonlinear-map /
flag-hold) **and** the MixedProbeTask, at T ∈ {128, 256, 512, 1024}:

| arm | params | **multi-cap fitness** |
|---|---|---|
| **WINNER (8M, CMA-tuned)** | **8,005,976** | **0.9614** |
| base8m hand-set (param-matched) | 8,075,048 | 0.9249 |
| base11m e98-spread-klr20 (E98 hand-set, reused) | 11,072,640 | 0.9231 |

- **+0.037 over the param-matched 8M hand-set** (equal 25/25/25/25 mixture,
  `knob_lr_mult=20`, `lam_max=1.5`, `beta_max=2.0`, depth 4 / 32 heads / N=32) —
  isolating the gain of the CMA TUNING itself at identical param budget.
- **+0.038 over the 11.07M E98 hand-set**, i.e. a **better multi-capability model
  at 0.72× the parameters**. The split-gate's +3.1M cost is not only amortised —
  the tuned 8M cell strictly dominates the untuned 11M one.

The gains concentrate **where it is hardest**: the state-nonlinear corner and the
mixed task, and especially at **long-context extrapolation** (T=1024).

## 3. What CMA discovered (and why it makes mechanistic sense)

The tuning is not a black box — every move it made aligns with the mechanics from
the prior studies:

1. **Optimal mixture is NOT uniform — starve the easy corner, feed the hard ones.**
   The winner allocates **track 0.40, count 0.28, nonlin 0.31, latch 0.009** (the
   coverage floor keeps latch at exactly 1 head). SPECIALIZATION_STUDY already
   flagged flag-hold/latch as "the least discriminating witness… solvable as soon
   as *any* heads hit the latch corner". CMA exploited precisely this: **one** latch
   head still yields latch ≈ 1.0 (0.977 at T=1024), and the freed capacity is
   redirected to the genuinely hard reflection (track) and state-nonlinear (nonlin)
   corners — which is exactly where the +0.09 (nonlin) and the long-context S5
   stability come from. This is a concrete, interpretable refinement of the
   "balanced 25/25/25/25 population" finding: balance is a safe *default*, but the
   capability-optimal placement is *skewed by corner difficulty.*

2. **High `beta_max` (2.75 vs 2.0) deepens the reflection.** The along-key
   eigenvalue is λ − β; the track/S5 corner needs β > λ. Giving β more headroom (and
   placing 40% of heads on track) is what holds S5 at **0.998 to T=1024** where the
   11M hand-set (β_max 2.0, equal mixture) collapses to 0.846.

3. **Low knob-LR (5.4 vs 20) + high base LR (1e-3 vs 3e-4): let the projections
   do the work, refine the placed knobs gently.** UNIFIED_LEARNABILITY found the
   input-dependent q/k/v/o projections absorb the task while the placed knobs need
   only mild refinement. CMA pushed the *projection* LR up ~3× and the *knob* LR
   multiplier down ~4×, consistent with that division of labour — the knobs are
   already placed on the corners, so they need nudging, not driving.

4. **Small state, many heads (N=16, H=60 vs N=32, H=32).** At a fixed 8M budget the
   recurrence cost is ∝ depth·dim·H·N; trading N=32→16 buys roughly 2× the head
   count, i.e. more independent capability slots and finer horizontal
   hybridization, with no loss on any corner (count/track/latch unaffected, nonlin
   improved).
