# UNIFIED-CELL LEARNABILITY — training ONE cell to self-organize onto the corners

**Task:** `unified-learnability`. The prior study
([UNIFIED_CELL_RESULTS.md](UNIFIED_CELL_RESULTS.md)) proved **expressivity** — the
single un-clamped matrix-recurrence kernel `S = φ(λS − β·k(kᵀS) + i·kvᵀ)`
realises every capability corner (track / count / latch / nonlinear) by changing
its per-head knobs, and the gain λ is the causal lever. But it also found a
**learnability gap**: a single LEARNED cell trained at T=128 from a *generic*
init did NOT self-organize onto the corners — its per-head (λ,β,γ) stayed near
init (λ never ≥1, the along-key eigenvalue never went negative), so the q/k/v/o
projections absorbed the trained-length task and the recurrence knobs got no
gradient pressure to reach the extremes.

This study tests the proposed fix, on the **same validated Triton kernel (no
rewrite)**:

1. **INIT SPREAD** — initialise the per-head knobs spread ACROSS the corners
   (round-robin: ¼ track, ¼ count, ¼ latch, ¼ nonlinear) so descent **refines**
   specialisation instead of discovering it from scratch.
2. **KNOB-SPECIFIC LR** — put λ,β,i,γ (the recurrence knobs) in a SEPARATE
   optimizer param-group at a higher LR (swept **1× / 5× / 10× / 20×** the
   projection LR; 1× = init-spread alone) so the knobs actually move.

REAL training only, fp32, schedule-free AdamW, ~7.93M params, depth 4, 32 heads,
N=V=32, idle-GPU-only scheduler. 3 seeds {42,123,456}; train T=128; eval (length
extrapolation) at T∈{128,256,512,1024}. **78/78 runs completed, 0 failures.**
`paper/main.typ` untouched.

## 1. What changed (implementation)

- **`ndm/models/unified_cell.py`** — `UnifiedCellLayer(spread_init=True)` partitions
  the H heads round-robin onto the four corners at init (free gain `lam_max=1.5`,
  `gamma_mix` φ so a per-head γ∈{≈0 linear, ≈1 tanh} selects linear vs saturating):

  | corner | λ | β | γ | along-key eig λ−β |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | track  | 0.9 | 1.8 | 0.05 | **−0.9** (reflection / S5) |
  | count  | 1.0 | 0.0 | 0.05 | +1.0 (pure integration) |
  | latch  | 1.3 | 0.0 | 0.95 | +1.3 (bistable ±1 / tanh) |
  | nonlin | 0.9 | 0.5 | 0.95 | +0.4 (state-nonlinear φ) |

- **`experiments/expressivity_tasks/train_hybrid.py`** — `--knob_lr_mult M` builds a
  second optimizer param-group holding every `lam_raw/beta_raw/igain_raw/gamma_raw`
  at `lr·M`, projections stay at base `lr`. (`--curriculum` length-schedule lever
  also added; not needed — see §5.)
- **`ndm/models/ladder_lm.py`** — new arm `unified-learned-spread`.
- **`experiments/expressivity_tasks/tasks/mixed_probe.py`** — `MixedProbeTask`: a
  REAL mixture where every sequence is a genuine instance of one of the four
  probe generators, mapped into disjoint token blocks of a shared vocab (no
  mocks). Forces one cell with shared knobs to serve all four capabilities at
  once — the cleanest specialisation witness.

The validated forward/backward kernel and the kernel-correctness gate
(`tests/test_unified_cell.py`, <1e-4) are untouched.

## 2. Headline — do the heads HOLD their corners or drift back to center?

**They HOLD. 100% of heads stay specialised; 0% drift to the generic center, on
every probe and at every knob-LR multiplier.** This is the exact opposite of the
generic-init cell, whose knobs collapsed near the center (λ̄≈0.95, β̄≈0.5, γ̄≈0.5,
0% reflecting heads).

The decisive view is the **MIXED probe** (all four capabilities trained
simultaneously). Grouping heads by their init corner and reading the *final*
per-head knobs (pooled over 96 heads/corner × 3 seeds), the four populations
stay cleanly separated and on-corner:

**Per-corner head-population, final knobs on the MIXED task** *(klr=10×)*:

| init corner | n | final λ | final β | final γ | final eig λ−β | regime held? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| track  | 96 | 0.918 | 1.813 | 0.089 | **−0.895** | ✓ reflection |
| count  | 96 | 0.981 | 0.001 | 0.053 | +0.981 | ✓ integration |
| latch  | 96 | 1.261 | 0.000 | 0.941 | **+1.260** | ✓ **gain>1** + tanh |
| nonlin | 96 | 0.818 | 0.586 | 0.941 | +0.232 | ✓ nonlinear φ |

The **latch population keeps λ≈1.26 (>1)** — precisely the un-cribbed regime the
generic-init learned cell *never reached* (it capped below 1). The **track
population keeps eig λ−β ≈ −0.9** — the negative along-key eigenvalue (reflection)
the generic-init cell never produced (0% reflecting heads → here a stable 25%,
exactly the track quarter). Descent *refines* each corner (e.g. nonlin relaxes λ
0.9→0.82, count nudges β→0) but never abandons it.

  (klr20) — ~5× the track preset (0.111) and essentially matched to LSTM (0.563),
  the strong learned state-tracker. The reflection that the generic cell never
  found is now present and *learned-refined*.
- **latch (flag-hold) — gap closed.** Generic collapsed at long gaps
  (0.531 @1024); spread holds the bit **perfectly to T=1024 (1.000)**, matching
  the latch preset, because the latch heads keep λ>1 + tanh.
- **count (aⁿbⁿcⁿ) — gap closed.** 0.836 → **0.898** @1024 (klr10), within 0.05
  of the count preset (0.920).
- **mixed (all four at once) — gap closed and beats LSTM.** 0.538 → **0.922**

  **wins** (0.850 vs 0.695); it lands 0.05 short of the anomalous linear-track
  best. No drift, no failure — a probe artifact, flagged honestly.

## 4. Which knob-LR multiplier worked?

- **At the trained length (T=128)** all multipliers (incl. 1×) saturate — spread
  init alone already puts every corner at its ceiling.
- **For length extrapolation (T=1024)** higher knob-LR helps **monotonically** on
  the hardest corners: S5 0.224→0.347→0.448→**0.541** (klr 1→5→10→20); mixed
  0.587→0.623→0.668→**0.719**. Count peaks at klr10 (0.898). The drift table
  confirms the mechanism — higher klr moves the knobs more (klr20 S5
  |Δβ|=0.085 vs klr1 0.014), and that extra motion is what sharpens the corner
  for out-of-distribution lengths.
- **Recommendation: knob-LR ≈ 10–20×.** klr20 is best for the hardest probes
  (S5, mixed); klr10 is marginally best for count. klr1 (init-spread alone) is
  enough to *hold* specialisation and to match presets in-distribution, but
  under-extrapolates — so **both interventions matter**: spread fixes *where the
  heads sit*, knob-LR fixes *how sharply they commit* for extrapolation.

---

## 5. Was a length curriculum needed?

**No.** Init-spread + knob-LR already (a) holds 100% corner specialisation with
0% center drift, and (b) closes the accuracy gap on track, count, latch, and the
mixed task at both the trained length and 8× extrapolation. The curriculum lever
(`--curriculum 128,256,512,1024`, train-length ramp) is implemented and available
in `train_hybrid.py`/`run_unified_learnability.py`, but the primary interventions
were sufficient, so it was not run. (The one partial miss — iter-map vs the
anomalous linear-track preset — is a probe-design artifact, not a learnability
failure curriculum would fix.)

## 6. Honest verdict

**Can a SINGLE cell be TRAINED to occupy all four corners — and what does it
take?**

**Yes — with the right initialisation, not the right capacity.** The expressivity
was never in doubt; the gap was that a generic-init learned cell had no gradient
incentive to leave the center at T=128. Two cheap interventions fix it
conclusively:

1. **Init-spread is the load-bearing fix.** Placing the per-head knobs on the
   corners at init makes descent *refine and hold* the specialisation rather than
   discover it. 100% of heads stay on-corner, 0% drift to center, even at base LR
   and even when all four capabilities are trained simultaneously (mixed task).
   The latch heads keep λ>1 and the track heads keep a negative along-key
   eigenvalue — the exact un-cribbed regimes the generic cell never reached.
2. **Knob-specific LR (≈10–20×) is the sharpening fix.** It lets the recurrence
   knobs move enough to commit each corner for *length extrapolation*, turning the
   in-distribution match into an 8× extrapolation match (S5 0.22→0.54, mixed
   0.59→0.72).

With both, the learned cell **matches or exceeds the corner presets** on track,
count, and latch, and **beats both the best preset and the LSTM on the mixed
all-capabilities task** — a single recurrence learning four distinct, stable,
specialised head-populations.

**Paper implication.** The prior honest headline was *"free the gain and one
kernel can be any of the four — but you must train it to want to."* This study
supplies the *"how"*: **the knobs are under-determined by short fixed-length
training, but they are not unlearnable — spread the init across the corners and
give the recurrence knobs their own learning rate, and a single cell
self-organises into a stable mixture-of-specialists that holds all four corners
at once.** Expressivity and learnability are both achievable in one kernel; the
missing ingredient was an inductive *placement*, not more capacity or a longer
schedule.
