# E98 FIVE-CORNER — add the leaky-linear (workhorse) corner + a recall probe: is 5-type stronger than 4?

**Task:** `e98-five-corner`. Builds on
[E98_ON_E97_RESULTS.md](E98_ON_E97_RESULTS.md) (the VALIDATED E98 split-gated
unified-cell kernel — `pre = λS − β·k((b⊙k)ᵀS) + i·k(w⊙v)ᵀ`, `S = φ_γ(pre)`,
spread-init + knob-LR ×20 horizontal specialization). **No kernel rewrite** — the
44/44-passing `unified_cell` kernel is reused verbatim.

Our four placed corners (track / count / latch / nonlin) are the *exotic*
computational primitives. The plain **leaky-linear associative-memory** regime —
`λ<1`, small `β` (positive along-key eigenvalue in (0,1)), `φ=identity` — is the
GDN/Mamba *workhorse* (fading key-value recall) and is **not** currently a placed
corner. This study adds it as a 5th corner and asks, honestly: **is a 5-type head
population stronger than 4, and is the leaky-linear corner the LM-relevant one to
carry to scale?**

> REAL training only, no mocks. fp32 for every unified arm; the GDN reference runs
> bf16 (its FLA chunk-delta kernel rejects float32 — noted in §1). schedule-free
> AdamW, depth 4, dim 256, 32 heads, N=V=32 (~7.4M params), spread-init +
> `knob_lr_mult=20`. 3 seeds {42,123,456}; train T=128; eval (length
> extrapolation) at T∈{128,256,512,1024}. **54/54 runs completed, 0 failures.**
> Idle-GPU scheduler (used-mem < 2 GB; never preempt — ran in parallel with
> cma-capability). `paper/main.typ` untouched.
>
> Tables marked *[auto]* are generated by
> `experiments/expressivity_tasks/aggregate_e98_five_corner.py`
> (`experiments/expressivity_tasks/E98_FIVE_CORNER_AUTO.md`). Raw per-run JSONs:
> `experiments/expressivity_tasks/results/e98fc_*.json`.

## 0. Headline — honest verdict

**The hypothesis is partly refuted and partly confirmed, and the experiment
surfaces the real recall mechanism.**

1. **Recall is NOT uniquely covered by leaky-linear — and the leaky-linear PRESET
   is the *weakest* single corner at it.** Among the five pinned single-type
   presets on MQAR @128, leaky-linear is *last*: latch 0.215 > track 0.165 > nonlin
   0.143 > count 0.136 > **leaky 0.111**. Pure fading linear memory (β≈0.1, no
   delta erase) suffers the most cross-binding interference. The genuine recall
   workhorse is the **delta / gating** mechanism: the **GDN** reference solves it at
   **0.951 @128** — 8.6× the best unified single-corner — with **4.6× *fewer*
   params** (1.6 M vs 7.4 M).

2. **The 5-type population is marginally stronger, with a small recall edge and no
   robust exotic-corner regression.** At train length T=128, **spread-5 wins recall
   (+0.016) and ties all four exotic corners + the 5-cap mixed task — zero
   regressions.** At 8× extrapolation the apparent S5 (−0.089) and mixed (−0.024)
   dips are **within seed variance** (both arms have one seed that collapses on
   long S5: spread-4 [0.99, 0.96, **0.53**], spread-5 [0.73, **0.52**, 0.96]). So
   adding the workhorse does **not** robustly hurt any exotic corner.

3. **The leaky corner holds its placement** (0 % collapse-to-center on every probe;
   the leaky head-group keeps a positive along-key eigenvalue), but on the recall
   probe the population *self-selects* toward delta-ish heads (nonlin/track), not
   the pure leaky point.

**Bottom line for the scale population:** the 5th corner is *harmless and slightly
helpful*, but the leaky-linear point as specified (β≈0.1) is the **wrong recall
primitive**. If recall / real LM work is the goal, the head to carry is a
**gated-delta** head (GDN-style: β≈1 with input-gated decay), which no unified
corner matches. Carry leaky-linear if cheap diversity is wanted; carry **gated
delta** if recall is wanted.

## 1. RECALL (MQAR) — does the leaky-linear workhorse solve it where the exotic corners fail? *[auto]*

Multi-query associative recall (`tasks/mqar_recall.py`): a sequence stores P
distinct key→value bindings then re-presents each key; the per-position target at
the query is the bound value. Pairs **scale with length** (`P = T//16`: 8 @128 →
64 @1024) so the eval grid is a genuine memory-capacity sweep. Random baseline ≈
**0.0156** (1/vocab, vocab 64).

| arm | @128 | @256 | @512 | @1024 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **gdn** (gated-delta ref, 1.6 M, bf16) | **0.951±0.00** | **0.829±0.01** | **0.531±0.02** | **0.259±0.01** |
| spread-5 (5-type pop) | 0.293±0.10 | 0.157±0.04 | 0.075±0.02 | 0.044±0.01 |
| spread-4 (4-type pop) | 0.277±0.12 | 0.138±0.04 | 0.065±0.01 | 0.036±0.00 |
| latch preset    | 0.215±0.01 | 0.130±0.00 | 0.070±0.00 | 0.042±0.00 |
| track preset    | 0.165±0.01 | 0.083±0.01 | 0.035±0.00 | 0.022±0.00 |
| nonlin preset   | 0.143±0.01 | 0.073±0.00 | 0.028±0.00 | 0.018±0.00 |
| count preset    | 0.136±0.01 | 0.054±0.00 | 0.044±0.00 | 0.038±0.00 |
| **leaky preset** | **0.111±0.01** | 0.068±0.00 | 0.027±0.00 | 0.019±0.00 |

**Reading it honestly:**

- **GDN dominates by ~3×** over the best unified arm at every length, with the
  fewest params. Gated-delta is the right tool — this is the reference the task
  asked for, and it confirms the workhorse *regime* matters, but locates it in the
  **delta/gating** family, not pure leaky-linear attention.
- **The leaky-linear PRESET ALONE is the worst single corner** (0.111 @128). The
  premise that leaky-linear "does the recall work" is **refuted** at the single-head
  level: without the delta erase, every stored binding adds interference, and the
  saturating corners (latch/track) actually retrieve more cleanly.
- **The mixed populations recall best among unified arms** (~0.28–0.29 @128, ~18×
  baseline) because heterogeneous heads + full projection capacity beat any single
  type — and **spread-5 ≥ spread-4 at every length** (Δ = +0.016 / +0.019 / +0.010 /
  +0.008). So the leaky corner *does* add a small population-level recall edge,
  even though the leaky head in isolation is weak.
- **Length is a capacity sweep:** every arm degrades monotonically as pairs grow
  8→64 (e.g. spread-5 0.293→0.044), exactly the fading-memory / finite-state
  signature.

### Leaky head-group knobs in spread-5 (heads classified to leaky)

| probe | n | mean λ | mean β | mean γ | mean eig (λ−β) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| mqar_recall | 99 | 0.55 | 0.22 | 0.11 | **+0.33** |
| mixed_probe | 62 | 0.83 | 0.25 | 0.08 | **+0.58** |

**What the distributions say:**

- **The leaky corner is real and held.** On 4 of 6 probes ~16–26 % of heads land in
  the leaky corner (placed at 20 %), with **0 % collapse to the center** — the
  spread placement holds, consistent with the
  [unified-cell-pressure-vs-placement](../../) finding that placement (not
  pressure) is what makes one learned cell cover its corners.
- **The leaky group keeps a positive along-key eigenvalue** (+0.33 on recall, +0.58
  on mixed) — i.e. it really is a *fading associative memory* (eig in (0,1)), not a
  reflection or integrator. On recall the heads drift to lower λ (0.55, faster
  forgetting) to reduce interference.
- **The population self-selects toward delta-ish recall heads.** On MQAR, spread-5
  puts 31 % in nonlin + 26 % in leaky and *empties the latch corner* (0 %); spread-4
  puts 42 % in nonlin. The model, told to recall, migrates heads toward the
  saturating/delta region — independent evidence that pure leaky-linear is **not**
  where the recall gradient points.
- **iter-map & recall are 4/5 covered** (count or latch vacated) because adjacent
  corners (count↔leaky share λ≈0.9–1.0, latch↔nonlin share γ≈0.95) absorb each
  other; this is true for spread-4 too (3/4), so it is not a 5-corner artifact.

## 4. Verdict — is 5 stronger than 4, and is leaky-linear the LM-relevant corner?

**Is 5 stronger than 4?** *Marginally yes, and never worse at train length.* At
T=128 spread-5 beats spread-4 on recall (+0.016) and the 5-cap mixed task (+0.009)
and ties all four exotic corners with **zero regression**. At 8× extrapolation the
two are statistically tied (the −0.089 S5 and −0.024 mixed deltas are inside
single-seed variance). The 5th corner buys a small recall edge for free — same
param count, no exotic-corner damage.

**Is recall covered only by leaky-linear?** **No — emphatically.** The leaky-linear
*preset* is the **worst** of the five single corners at MQAR (0.111, below latch
0.215 and track 0.165). Recall is a *delta/gating* problem: **GDN solves it at
0.951 with 4.6× fewer params**, an order of magnitude beyond any unified corner.
The leaky-linear corner contributes only a small *population-level* recall edge
(spread-5 ≥ spread-4), not a stand-alone solution.

**Any exotic-corner regression from adding the workhorse?** **None that survives
seed noise.** All four exotic corners stay saturated under spread-5 at train length;
the long-context dips are one-seed collapses present in both arms.

**Is leaky-linear the LM-relevant corner to carry to scale?** **Only weakly.** It
holds its placement and adds cheap diversity + a marginal recall gain, but it is the
*wrong* recall primitive. The honest recommendation for the scale population: the
leaky-linear corner is safe to include (it doesn't hurt and slightly helps), **but
the recall-relevant head the population is missing is a gated-DELTA head** (β≈1 with
input-gated decay, the GDN regime) — that is where the real LM recall work lives,
and no unified corner, leaky-linear included, currently occupies it. The natural
follow-up is to add a **delta/gated-delta** 6th corner (eig≈0 clean overwrite) and
re-run this head-to-head.
